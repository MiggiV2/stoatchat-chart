What to verify

Copilot’s first draft will probably be useful, but I would manually check:

    Service names and internal URLs match what Stoat expects in Revolt.toml and related env/config.

    Websocket routing on /ws works through Traefik ingress, since chat apps break subtly when this is wrong.

    Only 443 is exposed publicly, while MongoDB, Redis, RabbitMQ, and object storage stay ClusterIP/internal-only.

    Stateful pieces use persistent volumes and upgrades won’t recreate data stores accidentally.

I would also expect to rewrite probes, storage classes, and ingress annotations by hand, because those are exactly the spots where autogenerated charts often look plausible but fail in a real cluster.
Practical shape

The simplest maintainable result is usually:

    Chart.yaml

    values.yaml

    templates/deployment-web.yaml

    templates/deployment-api.yaml

    templates/deployment-files.yaml

    templates/deployment-pushd.yaml

    templates/service-*.yaml

    templates/ingress.yaml

    templates/configmap-revolt.yaml

    templates/secret-env.yaml

Then put Flux on top only after a local helm template and maybe a throwaway namespace test succeed, which matches a safer GitOps workflow for a cluster with limited resources. On your smaller K3s footprint, that also helps you avoid deploying a bloated chart that wastes RAM because Copilot mirrored Compose too literally.
